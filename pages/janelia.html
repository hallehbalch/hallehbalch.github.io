3D super-resolution microscopy
Collaborators: Luke Lavis (HHMI Janelia), Eric Betzig (HHMI Janelia)

Resolving nanoscale single molecule features in optically scattering materials requires high signal to noise ratios that are challenging to obtain. Photoactivated localization microscopy (PALM) is a powerful tool for localizing sub-diffraction limited areas in a fluorescently labeled sample but its application was limited to sub-micron sample depths. In complement, Besel beam plane illumination microscopy is a powerful diffraction-limited approach to limit optical excitation to a confined focal plane of illumination, dramatically reducing background noise.

I worked on extending molecular-resolution imaging to three-dimensional structures by developing a planar Bessel beam two-photon activation and linear excitation scheme, with tailored photoactivatable fluorophores, to generate superresolution PALM images within highly scattering organic and biological samples.
