Resources on Diversity Equity and Inclusion in Academia
(in no particular order)

  • American Institute of Phyiscs (AIP) TEAM-UP report The Time is Now, a guide to systemic change in physics and astronomy.
  • Responses to 10 Common Critisism of Anti-Racism Action in STEM, and an extremely-well referenced resource.
  • Forbes article 4 Ways that Scientists and Academics can Effectively Combat Racism .
  • How Gender and Race Stereotypes Impact the Advancement of Scholars in STEM .
  • Hot tips for avoiding gender bias in letter writing.
  • Project Implicit allows individuals to test themselves for unconcious bias.
  • Growing a Healthy Lab workshop slides created and facilitated by Christine Y. Chen.
  • Nature magazine article Diversity in science: next steps for research group leaders.
  • Particles for Justice references for racial equity and gender equity in physics and astronomy.
  • The American Physical Society (APS) LGBT Climate in Physics Report (2016).
  • Resources to promote diverse colloquia, seminars, panels, and invited reviews: The APS Women Speakers List and URM Speakers List
  • Effective practices for recruiting and retaining women in physics . Individual best-practices resources for retention and promotion of faculty, graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, and undergraduates.
